About Me

Hello there, I'm Ethan — I recently finished my Master of Engineering degree at the University of British Columbia, where I was part of the Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) program. During my master’s, I focused heavily on software engineering and machine learning, which has become a real passion of mine over the past few years.

My journey into machine learning started back in 2023, after I went through all three of Stanford’s ML courses on Coursera. Those courses really sparked my interest and pushed me to take additional ML-related electives during my undergrad, which eventually led me to specialize in ML during my master's. Since then, I’ve explored areas like reinforcement learning, large language models, transformers, and more.

I actually started programming way back in high school — I picked up JavaScript in Grade 11 (around 2017) and haven’t stopped coding since. These days, Python is my go-to language, but over the years I’ve worked with quite a few others too, including C, C++, Java, Assembly, and even Verilog. I love building things and solving problems through code, whether that’s in higher-level software or lower-level systems.

Over the past six years, I've been balancing university with work — I usually spent my summers either working in industry or taking extra courses. My work experience has mostly been in firmware and software development, giving me a solid foundation across the stack.

Interesting Courses
  • CPSC 340: Machine Learning and Data Mining:

    Prof. Jeff Clune and Mark Schmidt

    This course focuses on data analysis and machine learning techniques. Topics include: Data representation and summarization. Supervised learning with frequencies and distances. Data clustering, outlier detection, and association rules. Linear prediction, regularization, and kernels. Latent-factor models and collaborative filtering. Neural networks and deep learning.

  • CPSC 550: Advanced Machine Learning:

    Prof. Danica Sutherland

    This course is a continuation of CPSC 340. Topics include: Deep learning. Generative models. Latent-variable models. Markov models. Probabilistic graphical models. Bayesian methods.

  • CPSC 532: Causal ML:

    Prof. Mathias Lecuyer

    This covers foundational frameworks for causal reasoning and recent ML techniques that enhance causal analysis. Topics include: Fundamentals of Causal Inference (Rubin & Pearl Models). Core Techniques & Goals of Causal Analysis. Machine Learning for Estimating Heterogeneous Causal Effects. Advanced Methods in Instrumental Variable (IV) Settings. Evaluation of Causal ML Models.

  • EECE 570: Fundamentals of Visual Computing:

    Prof. Xiaoxiao Li

    This is a special topics course focusing on the foundations of computer vision and machine learning/ deep learning methods for computer vision tasks. Topics include: Computer Vision & AI Foundations. Medical & Healthcare Applications. Generative & Vision-Language Models. Security & Robustness in AI.

  • CPEN 522: Software Verification and Testing:

    Prof. Ali Mesbah

    This course looks at key principles, methodologies, and practical applications in software analysis and maintenance. Topics include: Software Verification Principles & Ethics. Static & Dynamic Analysis Methods. Testing Levels & Strategies (Unit, Integration, Regression). Advanced Testing Techniques (Fault-Based, Mutation, Data-Flow Analysis). Bug Checking, Debugging, & Automated Repair. Test Automation & Tool Development. Domain-Specific Software Testing (Web, Mobile, GUI).

  • CPEN 542: Cybersecurity:

    Prof. Simon Oya

    This course provides an introduction to security issues in various aspects of computing, including programs, operating systems, networks, and Internet applications. The course examines causes of security breaches and gives methods to help prevent them. Topics include: Cryptography. Access Control. Cryptography use cases. Program security. Network security. Data privacy.

  • EECE 568: From Exploring to Building Machine Learning Models:

    Prof. Matthew Yedlin

    This course focuses on the exploration of current techniques in modern machine learning and deep learning. Topics include: Large language models (LLMs). Stable Diffusion. Scaling laws. Transformers. Alternative architectures to transformers. Practical applications in computer vision. Time series analysis. Generative AI.

  • EECE 571: Advanced Topics in Deep Learning:

    Prof. Renjie Liao

    This course focuses on geometric deep learning and probabilistic deep learning. Topics include: Graph Neural Networks (GNNs). Transformers. Group Equivariant Networks. Large Language Models (LLMs). VAEs. EBMs. Flow Models. Diffusion Models.
